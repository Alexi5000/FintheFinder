## Source-backed note 5
Approved source excerpt: ISO/IEC 42001 is an international standard that specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) within organizations. It is designed for entities providing or utilizing AI-based products or services, ensuring responsible development and use of AI systems. ... ISO/IEC 42001 on the other hand is a management system standard (MSS). Implementing this standard means putting in place policies and procedures for the sound governance of an organization in relation to AI, using the Plan‐Do‐Check‐Act methodology. Rather than looking at the details of specific AI applications, it provides a practical way of managing AI-related risks and opportunities across an organization. It therefore provides value for any business or entity.

Sources: [ISO/IEC 42001:2023 - AI management systems](https://www.iso.org/standard/42001)

## Evidence Notes

- An AI audit trail should log everything needed to answer four questions about any AI decision: what data went in, what came out, what the system did with it, and whether it was allowed. The EU AI Act requires high-risk AI systems to automatically record events over their lifetime, highlighting the importance of compliance through detailed record-keeping. ([source](https://www.collibra.com/blog/ai-audit-trails-what-to-log-for-models-and-agents-and-how-a-command-center-captures-it))
- The regulation outlines specific requirements for AI systems, including the necessity for documentation and auditing practices that align with compliance standards—ultimately aiming to ensure accountability and transparency in AI usage. ([source](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=OJ%3AL_202401689))
- The framework outlines auditing tools and procedures for investigation and assurance teams, detailing best practices for data protection compliance and methodologies for auditing AI systems. ([source](https://ico.org.uk/media2/about-the-ico/consultations/2617219/guidance-on-the-ai-auditing-framework-draft-for-consultation.pdf))
- According to Article 19 of the AI Act, this provision ensures that providers uphold transparency and accountability in their AI systems, thereby facilitating oversight and compliance checks. ([source](https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-19))
- The standard outlines the implementation of policies and procedures necessary for the governance of AI usage and emphasizes the management of AI-related risks across organizations, which includes maintaining proper audit logs as part of compliance measures. ([source](https://www.iso.org/standard/42001))
